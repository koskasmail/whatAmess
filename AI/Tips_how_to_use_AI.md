# Tips how to use AI

## #1

It basically means this: **AI can’t magically know what you want unless you *teach it the pattern first*.**  
Not “teach” like a school teacher — more like **showing an example so it understands the style or rule you want it to follow**.

Think of it like this:

When your teacher gives you a math formula, you can suddenly solve a bunch of problems.  
AI works the same way — if you give it an example, it can follow the pattern.

---

## 🧠 High‑school explanation  
Imagine you tell your friend:

> “Write my homework like *I* usually write it.”

Your friend would say:

> “Okay, but show me an example of how you write.”

AI is that friend.  
If you want it to follow a style, a pattern, or a rule, you need to **show it first**.

---

## 📌 How to “teach” the AI (simple example)

Let’s say you want the AI to write definitions like your teacher makes you do in class.

You can “teach” it like this:

### **You:**
> Here’s how I write definitions:  
> **Example:**  
> *Photosynthesis – The process plants use to make food using sunlight.*  
>  
> Now follow that style and define: **Gravity**

### **AI:**
> *Gravity – The force that pulls objects toward each other.*

You gave it a pattern → it followed the pattern.

---

## 📌 Another example: teaching it how you want code explained

### **You:**
> When I ask about code, explain it like this:  
> **Example:**  
> “`for i in range(5):` means the loop repeats 5 times.”  
>  
> Now explain this line: `if x > 10:`

### **AI:**
> “`if x > 10:` means the code runs only when x is bigger than 10.”

You “taught” it the style.

## 🧠 What you wrote (the commands)
You listed these:

- **Follow this example**
- **Learn this pattern**
- **Use this style**
- **Copy this format**
- **Write like this**

These are basically ways of saying:

> “AI, look at what I’m about to show you, and then do the same thing.”

---

## 📘 Example of how to actually use them

### **You (teaching the AI):**
> **Follow this example:**  
> *Homework Summary:*  
> - *Topic:* Photosynthesis  
> - *Definition:* Plants use sunlight to make food.  
>  
> **Now do the same thing for Gravity.**

### **AI (after learning your pattern):**
> *Homework Summary:*  
> - *Topic:* Gravity  
> - *Definition:* A force that pulls objects toward each other.

You gave a pattern → the AI copied the pattern.

---

## 🎒 Another example (writing style)

### **You:**
> **Use this style:**  
> “In simple words, atoms are tiny particles that make up everything.”  
>  
> **Write like this** and explain electricity.

### **AI:**
> “In simple words, electricity is the movement of tiny charged particles.”

You showed the style → the AI matched it.

---

## 🧩 Why this works  
AI doesn’t “learn” like a human.  
It doesn’t remember forever.  
But **in the moment**, it can copy whatever pattern you show it.

It’s like giving someone a template and saying:

> “Fill this out the same way.”
