**You can’t fully remove all recovery methods from a Gmail/Google account and return to “password‑only” sign‑in.** Google requires at least one additional verification method to protect your account from lockout and unauthorized access. This is a built‑in security rule, not an optional setting.   [Appuals](https://appuals.com/reset-gmail-password-without-recovery-mobile-number/)

## 🔐 Why Google Doesn’t Allow “Password‑Only” Accounts
Google enforces at least one recovery or verification option because:

- It prevents permanent account loss if you forget your password.  
- It protects against unauthorized access and password theft.  
- It ensures you can prove ownership of the account.  

If you remove all recovery options, Google cannot verify you — which means you could lose the account forever.   [Appuals](https://appuals.com/reset-gmail-password-without-recovery-mobile-number/)

---

## ✅ What You *Can* Do (Closest to “Password‑Only”)
Here are the steps to minimize extra verification while still meeting Google’s requirements:

### 1. **Remove recovery phone number**
- Go to **Google Account → Security → Ways to verify it’s you**  
- Delete the phone number.

### 2. **Remove recovery email**
- In the same section, remove the recovery email.

### 3. **Turn off 2‑Step Verification (2FA)**
- Go to **Security → 2‑Step Verification**  
- Turn it **off** if it’s enabled.

### 4. **Keep only the minimum required verification**
Google will still require **at least one** of the following:
- A recovery email  
- A recovery phone  
- A trusted device already logged in  
- A security question (older accounts only)  

You cannot remove *all* of them. Google insists on at least one method.   [Appuals](https://appuals.com/reset-gmail-password-without-recovery-mobile-number/)

---

## ⚠️ Important Risk to Understand
If you remove too many recovery options and something happens (password forgotten, suspicious login, device lost), **you may never regain access**. Google support cannot override missing verification.   [Appuals](https://appuals.com/reset-gmail-password-without-recovery-mobile-number/)

# 📱 The Easiest Verification Options

## ✅ **1. Google Prompt (One‑Tap Confirmation)**
This is the simplest method for most people.

### How it works
- You sign in on any device.
- A notification appears on your phone: **“Are you trying to sign in?”**
- You tap **Yes** → Done.

### Why it’s easy
- No typing codes.
- Works on Android and iPhone.
- Very fast and secure.

### How to enable it
1. Open **Google Account → Security**.  
2. Go to **2‑Step Verification**.  
3. Choose **Google Prompt** as your main method.  
  [Digital Citizen](https://www.digitalcitizen.life/enable-disable-2-step-verification-google-account/)

---

## 🔐 **2. Passkeys (Fingerprint / Face Unlock Instead of Password)**
This is even easier than Google Prompt—no password needed at all.

### How it works
- You sign in.
- Your phone asks for **fingerprint**, **face unlock**, or **PIN**.
- You’re in.

### Why it’s great
- No passwords.
- No SMS.
- Works across all your devices.
- Extremely secure (FIDO standard).  
  [Google Safety Center](https://safety.google/intl/en_us/safety/authentication/)

### How to enable it
1. Go to **Google Account → Security → Passkeys**.  
2. Add your phone as a passkey.
