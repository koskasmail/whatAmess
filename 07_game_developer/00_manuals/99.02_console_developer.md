# 🎮 Step 4 — Choose Your Console Path  
Here’s how each console works for hobbyists:

---

## 🟩 **Xbox — Best for Students & Hobbyists**
Microsoft is the most open.

### How to start:
1. Install Unity or Unreal  
2. Join **ID@Xbox** (free indie program)  
3. Turn your retail Xbox into **Developer Mode**  
4. Test your game on real hardware  

This is the easiest console path.

---

## 🟦 **PlayStation (PS4/PS5) — Medium Difficulty**
Sony allows solo developers, but you must apply.

### Steps:
1. Join **PlayStation Partners Program**  
2. Wait for approval  
3. Get access to SDK + devkits  

They want to see a prototype or portfolio.

---

## 🟥 **Nintendo Switch — Hardest but Possible**
Nintendo is strict but not impossible.

### Steps:
1. Register as a **Nintendo Developer**  
2. Apply for Switch access  
3. If approved, buy a Switch Devkit  
4. Use Unity/Unreal with Switch export modules  

They usually approve:
- Students with a real project  
- Indie devs with a prototype  
- People who show commitment  

---

# 🎮 Step 5 — Build Your Console Version  
Once approved, you’ll get:
- SDK  
- Documentation  
- Console-specific tools  
- Export modules for Unity/Unreal  

Then you can start porting your PC prototype to the console.

---

# 🎮 Step 6 — Submit Your Game  
Each console requires:
- Age ratings  
- Technical checks  
- Store assets  
- A review process  

After that, you can publish on:
- Xbox Store  
- PlayStation Store  
- Nintendo eShop
