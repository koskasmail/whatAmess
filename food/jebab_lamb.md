
# kebab


#### en
```
Ingredients for 8 large kebabs:
700 g beef (preferably lamb)
300 g lard (lamb fat)
1 red onion
1 small onion
1 hot green pepper
4 cloves of garlic
1 tablespoon salt
1 tablespoon sweet red paprika
0.5 teaspoon ground black pepper
1 teaspoon cumin
1 teaspoon ground hot chili pepper
1 teaspoon sumac
0.5 bunch of coriander
1 tablespoon concentrated tomato paste
How to prepare:
1: It is important to use a ratio of 30% fat to the amount of meat. So if you want a kilo, you need 700 grams of meat and 300 grams of fat, otherwise the kebab won't last. You need to use a good, quality knife, so I chopped the meat with my Global knife very easily, I sliced ​​the meat, cut it and chopped it and the fat until it was properly bound. (I also have a blade guard for the knife that protects its blade!)
2: I chopped all the vegetables with the knife very easily, coarsely coarsely thinly thinly It's really important to use a good knife that will chop everything well, the red pepper, green pepper, purple onion, garlic, when finished I transferred them to a napkin and squeezed out all the liquids, this is very important so that they don't wet the kebab!
3: I added the chopped vegetables to the meat that I chopped earlier, I continued to chop and open the protein in it so that it would bind properly and I could make a kebab, I added sumac, red paprika, chili Spicy, cumin, salt, pepper and half a bunch of chopped coriander and I continued to chop until I got a fairly uniform mass of meat and vegetables.
4: Transfer to a bowl and let the meat sit for a few hours or overnight. Then take it out and with wet hands take a piece and skewer it on a long, thick skewer, wet your hands again and press the kebab a little to create the beautiful shape like in the attached video.
5: Place on the grill and be careful not to grill it too high so that it is cooked on the inside. That's all, serve with yellow rice like in Turkey, spicy green pepper, onion and tomato on the grill, chopped coriander, red onion with sumac and enjoy a dream bite! You can also roll it into a loaf!
```
